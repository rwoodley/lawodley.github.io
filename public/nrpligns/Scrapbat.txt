In a world dominated by human industrialization, many animals fall behind. Not the scrapbat. Evolved with a passion to collect shiny rocks and minerals, scrapbats have taken a liking to rummaging through junkyards and collecting spare metal. They use this metal to fortify their nests and make small tools, successfully incorporating human rubbage into natural environments. Scientists have begun researching to see if scrapbats could play a part in larger conservation efforts.
