Large, carnivorous lizards found in the jungle. Swingtraps hunt by hanging from tree branches and swinging down to catch prey when they least expect it. Their tails are three times the length of their body, and are filled with strong muscles to keep them tied up in the canopy. They have spent so long living high up in the trees that their arms and legs have completely atrophied. In areas with heavy deforestation, swingtraps struggle to navigate along the ground.
