While other animals navigate dark caves with echolocation or night vision, the waxelite has decided to simply make its own light. The glands atop its head create a natural form of wax, and a long, thick hair acts as the wick. With a quick strike of its wick against the natural flint formations in its caves, the waxelite sets its own head alight, guiding itself through the dark tunnels. There's probably a metaphor here for being your own guiding light in the world, and the waxelite took it literally.
