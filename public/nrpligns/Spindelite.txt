Nobody likes centipedes, right? Now take a centipede, make it twenty feet long, and give it a beaming flashlight for an eye. Congrats, you've got a spindelite! Found deep underground in cavernous ravines, the spindelite bores through the darkness in search of prey. Its body is covered in a tough, rocky exoskeleton, and its limbs are versatile and flexible. Lucky for humans, the spindelite's weakness is sunlight, so it rarely ever makes it to the surface.
