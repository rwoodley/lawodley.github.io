Fifteen feet in diameter, the ozodisk is a sight to behold. They loftily float through the upper atmosphere, filter feeding on airborne microorganisms. Their skin is impenetrable, protecting them from both the biting cold and predators. You're probably wondering: what hunts a giant, flying disc that lives higher in the air than any other creature on the planet? Great question! Scientists believe that the ozodisk's skin is a vestigial trait from back when it lived lower to the ground, but it has long since floated higher than anything that could ever hurt it… except planes.
