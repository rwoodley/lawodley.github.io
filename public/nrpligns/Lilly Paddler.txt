Few animals have mastered camouflage like the lily paddler. From the shore of a pond, their disc shaped heads look no different than any other normal lilypad. This is exactly what the lily paddler wants, for its expert mimicry serves both to dissuade predators and lure in prey. The flower atop its head (or "pad," in technical terms) emits a sweet aroma which draws in insects, frogs, and the occasional bird. However, the flower is not a flower at all, but actually a mouth. As soon as prey lands atop the lily paddler, it grabs them with its flower mouth and swallows them whole.
