Thermal vents are home to nutritious microorganisms, which the ventstrich has evolved perfectly to consume. Its entire body is adapted for temperature management and burn protection. Its face is tough and calloused, allowing it to drink scalding water without pain. Its back and head are covered in large steam vents, which release the excess heat from the water. Even its feathers are imbued with metal filaments to prevent them from catching fire. The ventstrich is, by all accounts, completely fireproof.
