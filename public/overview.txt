Richard Cathasach has lived a life of tranquility in the humble town of Nokford. Remote and closed off, Nokford is left mainly untouched by the political struggles of the greater world, allowing Richard to grow up in peace. However, things change when the arrival of a warmongering knight leads to Nokford’s destruction. Richard, along with his two best friends, are forced to abandon everything they know and set sail on the high seas. Together with a talking skeleton, a raucous hobgoblin, and a whole lot of pirates, the group must search for the one thing that can save their home. An ancient trident, lost to the sea.

Trident’s Keep is a great book for all ages, enjoyed by kids and adults alike. It is child friendly, and does not include swearing, extreme violence, or inappropriate content. The themes are nuanced and mature, and the world of the story is vast and complex. This creates a narrative which is entertaining for kids and thought provoking for young adults.
